How adenosine works
Caffeine doesn't
give you energy.
It hides your tiredness.
Adenosine is a chemical that builds up in your brain all day, making you feel sleepy. Caffeine works by blocking adenosine receptors — it doesn't reduce the adenosine, it just hides it.

When caffeine clears, all that adenosine crashes into its receptors at once. That's the 3pm slump.
Next: what this means for your sleep
The invisible damage
You fall asleep.
But you don't recover.
Research shows caffeine consumed 6 hours before bed reduces total sleep time by 1+ hour — even if you don't notice falling asleep later. It specifically attacks Stage 3 (deep) sleep, where your brain flushes toxins and your body repairs itself.
Research finding
"Caffeine 6 hrs before bed reduced sleep by 1 hour and reduced deep sleep quality measurably"
Drake et al., Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine, 2013

❌ Worst time #1
First thing

Immediately.
Your cortisol peaks naturally between 8–9am — it's your body's own alertness hormone. Caffeine during this window competes with cortisol, building tolerance faster and making the coffee less effective than if you wait.

Optimal window: 9:30–11:30am when cortisol dips.
